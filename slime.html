Drawing the maps

In 2010, researchers in Tokyo used slime mold to map out the Tokyo subway system, arranging oats in the pattern of station throughout the city. Inspired by this, In tried it out with a map of the united states, with oats placed on the location of 15 large metropolitan areas.


Constructing the box

The box - I made the box from acrylic sheets, connected with acrylic cement, and waterproofed with clear epoxy. The maps are taped to the bottom so they can be seen through the plastic, and cities are marked with red nail polish on the base of the box. The bottom is then filled with agar gel to keep the slime mold moist and mobile.


Slime progress by day

Day 1 - left maps start with mold in San Francisco, right maps start in New York

Day 2 - Mold on all four maps begins

Day 3 - Mold begins to make connections with nearby cities, bottom right mold makes it all the way to the west coast

Day 4 - Mold spreads to all nodes, and goes off the maps.

Day 5 - exploratory spread condenses into more established tunnels

Day 6 - Mold is overtaken by contamination and dies.

Failed first attempt

The first experiment failed, I had only one larger map, and the mold only made it to Los Angeles from San Francisco, before it was contaminated and taken over by outside mold. To give myself a better chance at success I decided to reattempt with four smaller maps.
